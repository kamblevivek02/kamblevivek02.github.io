## Summary

NIST has released the second draft of its AI Risk Management Framework, with expanded guidance on generative AI and third-party AI risks.

## My Analysis

Having worked on ISO 27001 implementations, I see a clear parallel between NIST's AI RMF and traditional risk management frameworks. The key difference is the emphasis on "explainability" – something most organizations aren't ready for.

The most practical addition is the new section on managing AI risks from third-party vendors. This mirrors the TPRM work I've done, but with AI-specific considerations like data lineage and model drift.

## Practical Implication

Start documenting all third-party AI tools your organization uses, including ChatGPT Enterprise, Copilot, or embedded AI in SaaS products. You'll need this inventory for compliance regardless of which framework you adopt.
